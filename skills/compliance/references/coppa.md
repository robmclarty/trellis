# COPPA — Children's Online Privacy Protection Act (US)

## Applicability

Applies when an online service is directed at children under 13, or when an operator has actual knowledge that it is collecting personal information from a child under 13. In the K-12 edtech context, COPPA typically applies when the software collects student data and some students are under 13.

## Core requirements

**Verifiable parental consent** — Before collecting personal information from children under 13, the operator must obtain verifiable parental consent. In the school context, the FTC has acknowledged that schools can consent on behalf of parents when the data is used solely for educational purposes.

**Privacy policy** — Must be clear, prominent, and describe what data is collected, how it's used, and disclosure practices. Must be accessible from the homepage and any page where data is collected.

**Data minimization** — Cannot collect more personal information than is reasonably necessary for the child to participate in the activity.

**Retention limits** — Personal information must be retained only as long as necessary to fulfill the purpose for which it was collected. Must be deleted when no longer needed.

**Security** — Must establish and maintain reasonable procedures to protect the confidentiality, security, and integrity of personal information collected from children.

**No behavioral advertising** — Cannot use personal information collected from children for behavioral or targeted advertising.

**Parental access** — Parents must be able to review the personal information collected from their child, have it deleted, and refuse further collection.

## Key considerations for K-12 edtech

1. Schools can provide consent on behalf of parents for educational use only
2. Data collected under school consent cannot be used for commercial purposes beyond the educational service
3. The vendor must provide the school with access to review and delete student data
4. Student data must be deleted when the school terminates the relationship
5. Marketing or advertising based on student data is prohibited
6. Third-party sharing requires either parental consent or falls within the school official exception

## Common compliance checkpoints for software systems

1. Age-gating or school-mediated access prevents direct data collection from children
2. Data collection is limited to what the educational service requires
3. No advertising or commercial use of children's data
4. Deletion workflows exist and can be triggered by schools or parents
5. Data retention policies automatically purge data when no longer needed
6. Security measures are documented and regularly reviewed
