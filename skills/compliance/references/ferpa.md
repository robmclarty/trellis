# FERPA — Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act

## Applicability

Applies when handling education records of students in US schools that receive federal funding. An "education record" is any record directly related to a student that is maintained by an educational agency or institution.

## Core requirements

**Consent** — Schools must obtain written consent from parents (or eligible students aged 18+) before disclosing personally identifiable information from education records, with specific exceptions.

**Directory information exception** — Schools may designate certain information as "directory information" (name, address, phone, dates of attendance, grade level) that can be disclosed without consent, provided parents have been given the opportunity to opt out.

**Legitimate educational interest** — School officials with a "legitimate educational interest" may access records without consent. The institution must define what constitutes a legitimate educational interest in its annual notification.

**Third-party access** — Third parties (including software vendors) may access education records under the "school official" exception if: (a) the institution has determined they perform a service the institution would otherwise perform, (b) the vendor is under the direct control of the institution regarding use and maintenance of records, (c) the vendor uses the records only for the authorized purpose, and (d) the vendor does not re-disclose PII without consent.

**Audit and record-keeping** — Institutions must maintain a record of each request for and each disclosure of personally identifiable information. These records must be available for inspection by the parent or eligible student.

**Data minimization** — While not explicitly mandated in the same terms as GDPR, the Department of Education guidance recommends limiting data collection and access to what is necessary for the stated purpose.

## Key fields typically classified as FERPA-protected

- Student name, ID numbers
- Parent/guardian names and contact information
- Grades, test scores, academic records
- Disciplinary records
- Attendance records
- Special education records (also IDEA-protected)
- Behavioral records (including hall pass logs that track student movement)

## Common compliance checkpoints for software systems

1. Data is only accessible to users with a legitimate educational interest
2. Access controls enforce role-based permissions matching the institution's policies
3. Audit trails exist for all access to student records
4. Data is not shared with unauthorized third parties
5. Parents/eligible students can request access to their records
6. Data retention and deletion policies are defined and enforceable
7. The vendor agreement (Terms of Service, DPA) addresses all FERPA requirements
